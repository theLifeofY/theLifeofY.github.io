Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Here are our answers to the questions that you will need to know to celebrate with us!

RSVP Deadline:

We ask that you please RSVP by July 25th, 2026. If you need to make any changes to your RSVP form, you should be able to do so.

Attire:

For Friday's Welcome Dinner, please wear casual to dressy casual attire.

For Saturday's Ceremony and Reception, please wear cocktail, formal, or cultural formal wear.

For Sunday's Brunch, please wear anything you are comfortable in. If you will be joining us for the hike, please wear hiking boots or shoes with good traction.

Footwear:

We recommend wearing shoes that you feel good in, and that allow you to dance the night away. Our ceremony and cocktail hour will also take place outside in the grass, so please keep this in mind when selecting your footwear.

Weather:

In late September at Silver Falls State Park, you can expect high temperatures around 78° and low temperatures around 48°. While we are expecting a nice, sunshine-filled weekend, there is also the potential for rain. Please make sure to bring layers for the evening, and an umbrella or rain gear in case of rain.

Parking:

There is limited parking directly at the Davidson Ranch, which is reserved for vendors, individuals who are camping, and family. Because of this, we will have a mandatory free shuttle service from the Smith Creek Village parking lot to the Davidson Ranch.

On Friday, September 25th, our shuttle service will run from 5:30pm-6pm with return shuttles starting at 8pm.
On Saturday, September 26th, our shuttle service will run from from 2:45pm-3:45pm with return shuttles starting at 9pm.

If you know that you will need special accommodations outside of these times, please let us know as soon as possible so we can make alternative arrangements.

Plus Ones:

Please see your invite for the number of seats we've reserved in your honor. All guests should fill out their own RSVP form so that we have an accurate headcount for our final numbers. If you have any questions, please reach out to us.

Kids:

Please note that aside from breastfeeding infants, this is a 21+ wedding.

Cellphone Service:

At Silver Falls State Park, cellphone coverage is limited, and those who use T-Mobile should expect to have no service at all while in the park's limits. There also will be no access to wi-fi within the park. Because of this, we ask everyone(!) to please make sure they have downloaded our wedding weekend cheat sheet, which contains all of the information you will need for the weekend.

  • Wedding Weekend Cheat Sheet

Cellphone-Free Ceremony

We will be having a cellphone-free or "no phones" ceremony. Please do not worry about taking photos during this time as we will have a professional photographer throughout the entire evening. All we ask is that you are present with us and plan to turn-off your phone before the ceremony.

Gifts:

Your presence at our wedding is gift enough, but if you would like to give us a gift, please see the "Wedding Registry" tab of our website for more specific information.

Accommodations:

If you are planning to stay in the park, you should hopefully have all of your accommodations booked and set. :) If you would still like to stay in the park or need housing recommendations for the weekend, please let us know!
